# Literature Matrix

This note maps the current bibliography to the manuscript's argument so future collaborators can extend the paper without rebuilding the evidence base from scratch.

## Cultural and historical button studies

- `Plotnick2018`: book-length history of pushing as pleasure, panic, and politics
- `PlotnickInterface2012`: early electric push-button genealogy
- `PlotnickWarfare2012`: remote-action and conflict rhetoric

## Affordance and experience

- `Gaver1991`: affordances as actionable relations
- `Norman1999`: conventions and perceived actions
- `McCarthyWright2004`: experience-centered interaction framing

## Pressing as curiosity, boredom, and agency

- `Wilson2014`: open-ended self-stimulation under low task demand
- `KiddHayden2015`: curiosity as uncertainty-driven exploration
- `HaggardChambon2012`: sense of agency
- `HallHenningsen2008`: social facilitation in HCI contexts

## Redness, salience, and reach

- `ElliotMaier2014`: psychological effects of color
- `GaoEtAl2007`: cross-cultural color-emotion variation
- `Li2008`: singleton-driven attention capture
- `CastellottiEtAl2022`: attention to information-optimal local features
- `Lin2021`, `Mirbagheri2024`, `Alghofaili2019`, `McNamara2019`: VR placement constraints
- `Bonin2022`: anthropometric grounding for arm's-length reach

## Embodiment, presence, and peripersonal space

- `CummingsBailenson2016`: immersive technology and presence
- `KilteniEtAl2012`: sense of embodiment in VR
- `Serino2019`, `SerinoEtAl2018`, `BrozzoliEtAl2010`: peripersonal space and action remapping

## Biofeedback and the study's physiological logic

- `LueddeckeFelnhofer2022`: review of VR biofeedback applications
- `RockstrohEtAl2019`: heart-rate-variability biofeedback in VR
- `ChittaroEtAl2024`: placebo-controlled breathing biofeedback in VR
- `MakowskiEtAl2021`: NeuroKit2 for simulated ECG

## Novelty and self-world relation

- `RanganathRainer2003`: novelty detection and memory
- `BarrettEtAl2015`: oneness-adjacent questionnaire grounding for the project's borrowed terminology
